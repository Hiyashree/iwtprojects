pigeon description :

Pigeons, also known as rock doves (Columba livia), are fascinating birds with a range of interesting characteristics: Appearance: Pigeons are medium-sized birds with a stout body, short legs, and a small head. They typically have a smooth, rounded appearance with a broad chest. Their plumage can vary widely in color, including shades of gray, brown, white, and even iridescent greens and purples on the neck. Urban pigeons, often referred to as "city doves," are usually a grayish color with two black bars on their wings. Size: Adult pigeons generally measure between 29 to 37 centimeters (11 to 15 inches) in length and have a wingspan of about 64 to 72 centimeters (25 to 28 inches). They typically weigh between 238 to 380 grams (8.4 to 13.4 ounces). Behavior: Pigeons are known for their strong homing ability, which has made them valuable as messenger birds throughout history. They can navigate over long distances and find their way back to their home lofts with remarkable precision. Diet: They are primarily granivores, meaning they eat seeds and grains, but they will also consume fruits and vegetables. In urban areas, they might eat food scraps and other human-provided food sources. Habitat: Originally, pigeons are native to rocky cliffs and coastal areas of Europe, North Africa, and South Asia. However, they have adapted well to urban environments and are now found in cities around the world. Reproduction: Pigeons are monogamous and usually form lifelong pair bonds. They build simple nests, often in ledges or building eaves, and both parents take turns incubating the eggs and feeding the young with a nutrient-rich substance called "pigeon milk," which they produce in their crop. Communication: Pigeons communicate through a range of vocalizations, including cooing, and use body language, such as puffing up their feathers, to convey messages to other pigeons. Pigeons are resilient and adaptable, which is why they've managed to thrive in diverse environments around the world.
